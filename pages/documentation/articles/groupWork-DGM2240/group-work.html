Teamwork makes the UX Dream Work!

March 30, 2022

5 minute read

Anyone who works in the Web Design and Development industry will certainly have to work in a group to produce a product. Our industry is collaborative by nature, and we need to practice the skill of working with others! It is a skill because there are some things you have to learn to be successful in collaboration. Such as the ability to communicate clearly with other team members. In this article, I’ll be outlining these key collaborative skills and the results of doing your part for the team.

Currently, I’m a part of a group that is in the process of redesigning a website. This is a very unique challenge for me because I haven’t had too many chances to work in a group with my peers. At the time that I’m writing this, I’ve been a part of a few groups that were conducting designs or redesigns; however, this group is by far the most engaging group I’ve been in, and I’ve learned a lot of skills that I think should be learned by anyone looking to join the industry. These skills include the ability to receive and give critiques to your peers, have clear communication with the group, and share ideas with the team.

Receiving/Giving Critiques

Any designer or developer should be able to give critique to their peers constructively and respectfully. The relationship you form with your peers is key to maintaining and giving critiques and is an important part of the collaborative process. Because how else can we learn or improve from our mistakes, if we didn’t have another's point of view on our work? Our team can constructively critique each other’s work. One time, during our project, I was presenting wireframes to my group. They all listened to my ideas and in the end, they didn’t put me down or toss my ideas to the side. They said what they liked and suggested some other solutions to try or communicated clearly to me why something wasn’t working. The result was me learning an important lesson about design principles and how others would incorporate a design principle into their design. Not only can this produce stellar projects, but it helps promote a learning environment that I argue is important to a project’s success.

Clear Communication

Another key component of working in groups effectively is having clear communication with your peers. Anything related to the project should be communicated to everyone in the group; this will help avoid any confusion and roadblocks that might hinder the group. Design goals, deadlines, group member whereabouts, and thoughts shouldn’t be lost in any confusion. However, when a group does experience confusion, it is key to recognize the current situation and get everyone on the same page. This job should fall to whoever is the project lead, but for this current group, I’ve been working with we don’t have a designated project lead. Rather, our group is composed of designers and developers that bring something to the table. In this situation, I’ve learned that clear communication is talking to each other in confidence about any issues that have popped up.

For example, we had a task to create a set of mobile wireframes for our project. The task was fairly simple, but our group didn’t have clear enough communication on what certain components should look like. Components such as buttons, headers, and padding dimensions weren’t properly defined by the team. Resulting in our wireframes differing drastically, and to remedy this we had to sit down again and revise our wireframes. This is a prime example of why communication is paramount in groups, especially in groups that are tasked with creating a design for a website or application. One of the prime design principles is Continuity, and because of the lack of communication, we failed to design a product with continuity. It was an easy fix at the end of the day, but it was an issue that could’ve been easily avoided with clear communication.

Sharing Ideas

Sharing or contributing ideas is a bit nerve-wracking to do, especially in the early phases of a group. For me, trying to muster up the courage to share an idea was difficult at first. If you are like me, my counsel to you is to remember that everyone is nervous to some degree to share ideas! It’s okay to share your ideas because every idea is an opportunity or option to improve the product your group is striving to create. One shouldn’t feel ashamed if their idea is not used in the end product, the whole process helps create better products. There are group activities that you can do like a Creative Matrix or a Round Robin exercise, both of which help stimulate the group to produce ideas. There are many other group exercises you can conduct to get the ideas flowing, but what is key is to participate and do your part for the group.

Currently, my group is still producing designs for the our client. But, I wanted to share a screen capture of Figma. This application is been the number one tool for us to share our ideas and iterate upon them. Which makes it our best collaborative space.

Conclusion

The three collaborative skills I mentioned are what I believe to be crucial skills to have. To be successful, each member should be able to demonstrate these skills. The team that i’ve been working with to redesign a website, has taught me these important skills. It has made the process rewarding not only for the client but also for ourselves as designers and developers.
