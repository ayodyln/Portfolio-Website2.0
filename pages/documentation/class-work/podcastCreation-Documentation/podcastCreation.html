Podcast Creation

The Games & Lore Podcast

Introduction

The goal of this project was to learn and explore the podcast creation process. Albeit my podcast isn't the best example of a podcast, I still learned the fundamentals of podcast creation and where podcasts came originally came from. Another goal was to understand what Immersive Authoring is and how it has an impact on someone.

I wanted to discover how to even talk into the microphone, whilst I was alone! This was by far the biggest obstacle I had to get past. Before I started this project, I had been contemplating how to speak into the microphone. Because I already have a fear of public speaking and I thought there would be a similar issue when I eventually started to create my podcast. To counteract this, I tried to pick a topic to make a podcast about that I truly enjoy and would feel a more comfortable rant about.

So, the Games & Lore Podcast was created. I felt that talking about video games was a good fit and I knew I would've enjoyed the process because I felt more comfortable talking about something I love.

Production Process

The production process was easy in the terms of me already having the proper equipment to record a podcast. I had access to recording software, I opted for Garage Band though, and I also have the Elgato Wave:3 microphone for audio input. But since I utilized Garage Band, I was limited to using my iPad Pro (5th Gen) because I don't have any Mac computers--this was proven to not be an issue.

I also used my iPad for creating my Podcast cover art. I utilized mainly one app to accomplish this, but it is fair to mention I used the Sketchbook app for the first sketch. I then utilized the Procreate app and just iterated upon my two favorite designs. It is not the best art, but because of the iPad I was able to create this work and I'm still learning how to use the Apple Pencil to draw. Below you can see the final drafts from the Procreate app.

I started with understanding what Garage Band is and if I'm even able to use my microphone with the iPad. Once I had Garage Band downloaded, I started to practice with the software and was trying to understand how to just record crisp audio. Luckily, my microphone was compatible with my iPad and I was able to record what I was saying. Now all I had to do now was decide how I was wanting to produce my podcast.

I started to tamper with a script and use that to provide structure to my podcast. However, once I had completed a draft I attempted to record a sample of my podcast and to see how the podcast flowed, which didn't flow well at all. I believe my issue was that I was reading the script word-for-word essentially and that resulted in me sounding like a mono-tone robot. So, I repurposed the script as a "guide" that helped me keep track of where I was and this proved to be more natural to me. I was able to rant from my thoughts and didn't feel like I was restricted to the script. So, I was now talking from my own will and it felt more free and comfortable to just talk and not give a speech that is following a script.

So, now it was just up to me to record my podcasts episode, following a loose script and mainly relying on my solo-bantering about the topic of the episode. Admittedly, there were still complications throughout the process. There were points where I trailed off on a tangent or lost my train of thought and to fix that I had to re-record portions of the podcast.

Testing and Iteration

My testing process was as follows:

  • Record Audio
  • Play audio back and fix if need be
  • Test audio levels on multiple listening devices
  • Test FX on intro/outro music and adjust audio levels
  • Playback the entire Podcast on my iPad
  • Upload podcast to Anchor.fm and listened to the audio via their website

I did testing this way to ensure that my audio wasn't a mess and to make sure I was speaking too. Then I had to account for all the extra music audio I was adding and make sure my recorded audio wouldn't conflict with the music. Ultimately, this helped create the intros and outros to add character to the podcast and help captivate my audience.

All this testing was partly iterating upon my work as well. Because each time a test failed it called for some type of iteration to improve my work, for example, my audio recording or my speech patterns required some form of iteration. I tried different recording environments adjusted the gain on my microphone, tried different music intros/outros, tried to speak with more emotion rather than my usual monotone voice, and tried different audio formats to make sure that my master the file was at least amazing quality. After all that, I eventually settled on my podcasts and worked on publishing my final podcast drafts.

Conclusion

To sum up, what I learned during this podcast project, I learned that immersive authoring is not an easy thing. It takes thought, time, and practice to make a quality experience for a consumer. Even though my podcast isn't the best and won't be hitting any top charts any time soon, it still helped me understand the podcast production process at a fundamental level. There are many other skills I think that are required too. For example, the ability to speak into the microphone for an extended period is a primary skill and was for sure my primary obstacle to overcome.

Is there anything I could've or would do differently as I look back on this project? Yes there is a few things: better recording environment, used a desktop application for recording, and worked harder to obtain music that relates to the video game I was talking about.

For my environment, it lacked in sound dampening and because of this, I had to mess with my noise suppression settings in Garage Band and it was troublesome to get it exactly right. For the desktop application, I feel I would've got my podcasts done a lot quicker if I just used my desktop instead of my iPad and that's just because I'm more efficient with my work on a desktop in general. Finally, the music issue, which was more of me having the conflict with figuring out how to obtain music from a video game legally and I opted not to do that because I didn't want to have copyright issues--also the Garage Band app wouldn't let me use the game music as a loop for recording, since it wasn't legally mine.
