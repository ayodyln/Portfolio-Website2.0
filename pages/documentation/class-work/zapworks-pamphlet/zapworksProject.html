Zapworks Pamphlet - Elden Ring

April 19, 2022

8 minute read

One of the most well-received video games in the past few years has finally been delivered to the world. This game is called Elden Ring, and it has taken the gaming community by storm. This is due to how the world is throw a person into a seemingly ever-expanding story. Some challenges test the will of a person to beat an AI in a video game! The company that brought this game to the world has also developed/produced Dark Souls, which is a notorious game that is incredibly difficult and is not for the faint of heart. This is what I designed a pamphlet about, with the purpose to help introduce the world to new players to this genre of the video game. To add a dash of immersive authoring of my own, I'll utilize some Zapworks tools to incorporate some AR features.

What is Zapworks?

Zapworks is a tool that helps designers bring augmented reality (AR) to print via Zap Codes/QR Codes. What this application offers is a set of tools that vary in intensity, such as simple QR links that open a website or complicated AR components of images in a 3D space. These tools enable someone to introduce some fun ways to story tell and bring a design to life in my opinion.

The goal of the pamphlet

This new game has encapsulated my imagination, which is a feeling I haven't experienced since playing Halo (CE, 2, 3). It felt like I was in a world you would only be able to imagine if you were reading a book! Which is a great sign of how this game has taken its story to the next level along with how it enabled a player to interact with the video game's world. Because of how stellar this game is and how well it immerses people, I wanted to make a pamphlet that helped introduce people to the video game. To accomplish that, I used zapworks and came up with these AR features to help add some creativity to the pamphlet:

  • Moving title image with the games menu theme music
  • Photo Album of in game content
  • Interactive World Map of the video game
  • Videos about the game

How I used Zapworks

To incorporate Zapworks with my pamphlet, I utilized two tools of the four that are offered from Zapworks: Designer (Beta) and Designer (Legacy). Both essentially serve the same purpose, but the beta version of the tool is more akin to Adobe Aero. Each tool is functional with a normal QR code or the Zapper code but offers slightly different experiences. The reason why I used two versions of the Designer tool was of some limitations that Zapworks was presenting.

For the beta version of the tool, I was using some images I found and edited to fit within the Zapworks image file constraints: JPEG or PNG and max 10 MB file size. However, the Zapworks web app would freeze constantly upon upload of the anchor image, and processing of the image will stall, ultimately the app will be in a frozen state. Because of this issue with uploading my desired anchor image, I had to resort to the legacy version of the Designer tool.

To interact with the pamphlet, the Zapper app is required to scan the Zapcodes.

Conclusion

The goal of completing the pamphlet was to learn how to incorporate AR into a print format, which is a form of immersive authoring. That extra level of storytelling helps establish a fun experience for a user. Since we have technology that helps bring the digital world to our fingertips, such as AR from Zapworks. We can add not only some style to our work but helps provide a memorable experience to someone, resulting in the overall success of a product. The pamphlet I came up with I feel was a good way to bridge digital with print and make it about something I am interested in. Albeit, the pamphlet I came up with is fairly simple. It still is a good example of creating an immersive experience for a reader of a print pamphlet.
